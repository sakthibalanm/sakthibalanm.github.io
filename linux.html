Linux

I have been a redhat linux user since 1998 and I love to work in linux eventhough it is difficult to install in systems and virtually very frustrating to look for suitable drivers for some hardwares; for me this itself is a fun and it also makes me to learn more about systems. But the new versions of redhat has been really good in recognizing the hardwares except for some cases like display problems in very few systems.  Well it has come a long way from redhat linux 5.x, 6.x and 7.x . I still remember those days where we had to install in the text mode and virtually do all the installs of drivers manually; those complete installations took few days sometimes weeks to make the system workable. But nevethless at least for me (I think for many) it is worth wasting time on these to get a more stable OS on our systems. Since 2004 I have been using Dell Inspiron 1000 notebook. Although I have worked in Linux for many years I am not an expert but will try to give you my experience here in installing Fedora core 2 and Fedora core 4 in my notebook.
